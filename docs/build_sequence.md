# Build Sequence — La Quebrada Viva House

**Status:** living document. Authoritative real-world build order for the cob/bottle house. Designed so a small site team (2 cob builders, 1 carpenter, 2 helpers) can execute it in 9–11 months, working only the dry season.

This is not the *render* build sequence — that lives in `build_scene.py`. This is the physical construction sequence on site, so Wesley can plan budget, labor, and material delivery.

## Pre-conditions before any earthwork

- Approved municipal anteproyecto (Escobar municipality).
- Soil test (granulometry + plasticity index) at the building pad and at the cob-borrow pit.
- Sieve test of borrow-pit soil: target 15–30% clay, 10–30% silt, 40–75% sand-gravel.
- Water source confirmed (spring above the cliff or the stream pool with filtration).
- Sand and lime delivered (or local lime kiln contract).
- Lapacho timber felled and stored under cover for ≥4 weeks pre-construction; FSC papers archived.
- Boleto privado executed; escritura traslativa stamped (target 2026-06-27).

## Phase 0 — Site preparation (weeks 1–3)

1. Survey + stake out building pad and footprint.
2. Mark contour drainage trench upslope of pad.
3. Clear vegetation in a 6 m radius around the building footprint. Save useful timber.
4. Cut and stockpile 8 m³ of cob mix base soil from the upslope borrow pit.
5. Stack 600 wine bottles (washed, label-stripped). Lapacho offcuts pile near south wall.
6. Dig the perimeter foundation trench to 60 cm depth × 50 cm width.
7. Lay French drain stones + perforated pipe in the trench bottom; daylight to the downhill cliff.
8. Cure the trench overnight to check drainage.

## Phase 1 — Stone foundation (weeks 4–6)

Critical: Rule 4 — earthen walls never touch ground.

1. Lay 30 cm of crushed-stone base inside the trench, lightly tamped.
2. Build dry-stacked stone footing 60 cm above grade using the on-site quartzite and sandstone.
3. Run a 20 mm-thick lime-mortar bedding layer on top of the stone.
4. Embed a continuous PE waterproofing membrane.
5. Place anchor bolts at door frames and at the four corner-post locations for the corredor.
6. Pour a 5 cm lime-stabilized base for the corredor; finish with broom texture.

## Phase 2 — Lapacho corner posts + roof framing (weeks 7–10)

Constructing the roof BEFORE the cob walls is the secret of cob durability: the walls cure under roof.

1. Erect 6 lapacho posts (4 corners + 2 mid) on the stone footing. Plumb and brace.
2. Install collar ties, ridge beam (30 × 20 cm lapacho).
3. Lay rafters at 60 cm o.c. — 15 × 8 cm lapacho.
4. Sheath roof with cana brava cane mat + 3 cm rough cob screed (insulation layer).
5. Lay sod-roof EPDM membrane, drainage mat, soil 15 cm, sod plugs.
6. Solar steel frame mounted separately, NOT on sod. Rule 9.

By the end of Phase 2 the building has a roof but no walls. Cob is built inside that umbrella.

## Phase 3 — Cob mixing + base walls (weeks 11–18)

The slow phase. Cob is the limiting reagent.

1. Mix recipe per bag: 1 part clay-rich soil, 2 parts sand, 1.5 parts straw chops 5–10 cm.
2. Tarp-mix with feet (faster than pugmill at this scale; cheaper).
3. Lay the first 30 cm cob "cake" on the stone footing. Trowel level on top.
4. Embed first row of bottles, neck pointing IN, label side already removed.
5. Build 6 lifts per week of 25 cm each. NEVER more than 40 cm wet at one time.
6. Trim walls daily with a machete after they leather-set (~6 h).
7. Sculpt window and door reveals as you go (Rule 1 — no right angles).

Window cuts: leave roughed openings 5 cm larger than the lapacho frame on all sides. Frames go in AFTER cob cures (Phase 5).

## Phase 4 — Wall completion, bottle features, niches (weeks 19–22)

1. Finish the bottle wall (west face) — the show-piece. Bottles in a Fibonacci-spaced grid for morning light effect.
2. Build interior niches, shelves, sculpted ledges.
3. Form-in the integral cob bench on the corredor.
4. Sculpt the door reveals to a final smooth curve.
5. Cure walls covered with damp burlap for 14 days. NO direct sun on wet cob.

## Phase 5 — Carpentry, windows, doors (weeks 23–26)

1. Plane and finish lapacho window frames (single-glazed except west façade; Low-E elsewhere — see `lqv/house/window_specs.py`).
2. Fit windows with low-expansion cob mortar (NOT cement).
3. Hang lapacho doors with hand-forged iron hardware.
4. Install corredor balustrade — turned lapacho posts at 15 cm o.c.

## Phase 6 — Lime plaster + finishes (weeks 27–30)

Critical: Rule 2 — lime only, never cement.

1. First coat: thrown lime "splatter dash" with horse-hair fiber. Cure 5 d.
2. Second coat: 8 mm hard-trowel lime + sand 1:3. Cure 7 d.
3. Third coat: 3 mm lime wash with iron-oxide pigment to match `#C8A580` honey-tan.
4. Mist for 7 d post-final coat. Lime needs water to set.

## Phase 7 — Services rough-in + systems (weeks 31–36)

1. PV array on steel frame (Rule 9), south-facing 18°, 5 kWp.
2. LiFePO4 battery bank in covered service shed downhill.
3. Micro-hydro intake at the upper stream weir (Rule 7).
4. 25 mm potable PEX run from spring or filter rig.
5. Greywater split: kitchen / bath outlets through grease trap → constructed wetland.
6. Blackwater to twin-chamber composting toilet vault.
7. Mosquito-mesh on EVERY cistern overflow (Rule 10).

## Phase 8 — Interior fit-out (weeks 37–40)

1. Polished red brick floor on lime-stabilized base.
2. Lapacho built-ins: kitchen counter, shelves, bed platforms.
3. Tatakuá built in the corredor's southeast corner. Hooded for smoke draft.
4. Tile bathroom in local ceramic; rainshower from PV-heated header tank.
5. Soft furnishings (hammock, ñandutí runners, aó po'í cushions, mate set).

## Phase 9 — Landscaping (months 9–10)

1. Stream pool weir maintenance + flat-rock pavers.
2. Footbridge with stone abutments (already in scene; STATUS task #2 completed).
3. Lapacho planting + petal-zone groundcover (Variant A condition).
4. Pindo palms in lower terraces (3–5 specimens, well spaced).
5. Mango canopy upgrade — 1 mature transplant + 2 saplings.
6. Kitchen garden: yuyos rack near corredor, maize and mandioca plot below pool.
7. Beehive (melipona) on the east cob wall, kissed by morning sun.

## Phase 10 — Habilitación + handover (month 11)

1. Final municipal inspection.
2. SENATUR alta as turistic alojamiento (if rental).
3. SET RUC + facturación rig (Wesley sole prop or SRL).
4. Operating manual handover (electric system, hydro shutoff, plumbing schematic).

## Critical path & risk register

| Risk | Phase | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Cob cracking from sun on wet walls | 3, 4 | Wet burlap covering daily. Skip 12:00–15:00 trowel sessions. |
| Roof timber warping if cob delays | 2, 3 | Pre-dry lapacho 4 weeks; provisional tarp ridge. |
| Lime burn injuries | 6 | PPE mandatory; potable water on site. |
| Water-table flooding the trench | 1 | French drain test before pour; trench cured overnight. |
| Stream weir washout in wet season | 9 | Stagger weir build to October–December dry stretch. |
| Permit delays at municipality | pre-build | Wesley to retain Paraguayan arquitecto colegiado early; do not wait until escritura. |
