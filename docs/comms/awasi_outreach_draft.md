## 0 · Why Awasi specifically

[Awasi Iguazú](https://www.awasiiguazu.com/) sits inside the **last continuous fragment of the Atlantic Rainforest in Misiones (AR)**, ≈90 km across the Paraná from La Quebrada Viva's Mbopicua cluster in Escobar, PY. Same biome (Paranaense / Atlantic Forest), same Relais & Châteaux positioning bracket, same EU-source multilingual guest profile (DE/FR/NL/EN), same selling premise — that the forest itself is the product. They are not a competitor for LQV because:

- Awasi runs 14 villas at ~USD 1,500–2,500/night for 2 pax all-inclusive with private guide+vehicle. LQV Phase 1 is 4 cabins targeting a softer price band with a restaurant + events line.
- Their bottleneck is the **AR side** of the Atlantic Forest. Ours is the **PY side** — same biome, different national park gravity (Mbaracayú vs Iguazú), different access logistics (Asunción airport vs Iguazú airport).
- A cross-biome itinerary — Iguazú falls + Awasi forest immersion + a Paraguay-side stop at LQV (German-Paraguayan culinary angle, family-feasible cabins, salto + quebrada) — is a multi-night package neither side can sell alone today.

**What we are asking for (in priority order):**

1. **Soft referral pipe.** Awasi's concierge can mention LQV as a "Paraguay-side extension" to guests who fly out via ASU rather than IGR.
2. **Operational mentorship channel.** Informal — once a quarter, 30 min — on R&C onboarding, EU agency contracting, multilingual staff training, naturalist-guide model.
3. **Co-marketed itinerary trial.** A 3-night Awasi + 2-night LQV package once Phase 1 opens, marketed jointly on both sides' channels.

We are **not** asking for capital, equity, branding licensing, or staff sharing. We respect Awasi's R&C exclusivity terms (only one R&C property per micro-region) — LQV will not pursue R&C membership in any geography that overlaps with Awasi Iguazú's catchment.

## 1 · One-page positioning blurb (English)

> **La Quebrada Viva — Paraguay-side Atlantic Forest, German-Paraguayan kitchen, family-scale architecture.**
>
> Sixty-two hectares of mature Paranaense Atlantic Forest in Escobar, Paraguay — a 60 km drive from the Brazilian and Argentine borders, two and a half hours from Asunción International. The escritura was signed on 2026-06-27. The buildable cluster sits on a 30.9-hectare contiguous polygon in Mbopicua, with NDVI 0.917 (wall-to-wall mature canopy, no clearings, no detected standing water inside the polygon) and a GEDI-measured canopy height of 27.7 m — the closest spaceborne lidar shot we have to the site reads as upper-tier primary forest. The remaining ~32 ha read as forest reserve.
>
> Topography is a classic quebrada: 73.5 m of relief across the parcel, 4.28 ha of flat ground (<8 % slope) anchoring Phase 1, and 71.6 % south-southwest aspect favouring cool morning rooms and valley-view terraces. Climate is Cfa humid subtropical, 22 °C mean annual temperature, 1,736 mm/year, no pronounced dry season (ERA5 1990–2025).
>
> **The build.** Phase 1 is four cabins, designed to a ten-rule LQV charter: 90 cm overhangs, passive cooling under 35 °C, laterite-and-stone palette, lapacho-petal carpets in season, hero views aligned to the natural pool and footbridge. Materials are local; the architecture sits below the canopy line. A small restaurant and event line are built into the master plan; the kitchen blends Paraguayan ingredients with German-Paraguayan technique (the partners are of Dutch and German-Paraguayan extraction; Asunción's German-speaking community has a 144-year continuous presence in this corner of the country).
>
> **The guest.** Couples and families who want the Atlantic Forest **without** the Iguazú gravity — quieter, slower, more domestic-feeling. The natural pool and the salto on the property are the centrepieces of the day. Birding and herpetology are abundant. The 144-year-old German-Paraguayan migration pattern between Asunción and San Bernardino gives us a year-round domestic shoulder.
>
> **The ask of Awasi.** A soft referral relationship — guests who fly into Iguazú and out via Asunción could extend two or three nights with us on the Paraguay side of the same biome. We would not market against Awasi Iguazú in any segment. Once Phase 1 opens (target 2027), we would propose a co-marketed multi-night package.
