## FX assumption (added 2026-06-10, UPGRADE_PLAN T0.5)

- **Indicative rate used in PYG columns above: 7.300 PYG / 1 USD.** This is a planning rate for budgetary take-off, not a transaction rate.
- **Refresh on the day of purchase commitment.** The Paraguayan guaraní vs USD rate has historically tracked the BCP (Banco Central del Paraguay) tipo de cambio referencial published daily at `www.bcp.gov.py` → "Cotizaciones del día". Cross-check against `melizeche/dolarPy` (open-source aggregator of casas de cambio rates published in Asunción) and the local casa de cambio rates published on `cambioschaco.com.py` and `maxicambios.com.py`.
- **Volatility band 2024–2026:** roughly 7.100–7.500 PYG/USD. Any single-line item whose USD subtotal exceeds 1.000 USD should be re-priced at the commit-day rate before issuing a purchase order (Section 6 PV/battery/inverter/hydro and Section 9 arquitecto/contingency are the high-leverage rows).
- **Footnote for Wesley:** local suppliers in Paraguarí, Asunción, Itauguá, and Caaguazú will quote in PYG; imported equipment (Section 6 LiFePO4 battery, Section 6 Pelton turbine, Section 6 composting toilet kit, Section 6 smart-home hub) will quote in USD or EUR. The PYG column above is the budget envelope; the USD column is the negotiation anchor for imports.
- **What this column is NOT.** It is not a hedge plan. It is not a quote. It is not a contract price. It is the same USD number above multiplied by 7.300 and formatted with Latin thousands separators (`.`) so Wesley can hand the doc to a PY accountant without conversion friction.
